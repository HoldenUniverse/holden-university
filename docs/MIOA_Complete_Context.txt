SECTION 4: DAMAGE MECHANISMS LIBRARY — 32 MECHANISMS (API 571)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Coverage: Every API 571 mechanism taught in Division 5 — Damage Mechanisms Academy

   1. General / Localized Corrosion
      Uniform or localized metal loss from electrochemical attack — the baseline thinning mechanism tracked at every CML.
   2. Pitting Corrosion
      Highly localized deep pits, often under deposits or from chlorides — dangerous because volume loss is small but penetration is fast.
   3. Crevice Corrosion
      Accelerated attack in shielded gaps (gaskets, deposits, threads) where stagnant electrolyte concentrates.
   4. Galvanic Corrosion
      Accelerated corrosion of the less-noble metal where dissimilar metals are electrically coupled in an electrolyte.
   5. MIC (Microbial)
      Microbially influenced corrosion — SRB and other organisms drive aggressive, often localized pitting under biofilms.
   6. CUI — Corrosion Under Insulation
      Hidden external corrosion beneath insulation in the 10-175 deg C range; one of the most costly and under-detected mechanisms.
   7. Erosion / Erosion-Corrosion
      Mechanical removal of metal (and protective films) by high-velocity or particle-laden flow, often at elbows and reducers.
   8. Flow-Accelerated Corrosion (FAC)
      Dissolution of the protective oxide layer in wet steam / condensate systems — a major power and utility piping risk.
   9. Naphthenic Acid Corrosion (NAC)
      High-temperature corrosion in crude units driven by naphthenic acids (TAN) and velocity.
  10. Sulfidation
      High-temperature corrosion by sulfur compounds in hydrocarbon streams; governed by the Modified McConomy curves.
  11. CO2 Corrosion (Sweet)
      Carbonic-acid attack in wet CO2 systems — a primary mechanism in production and gas gathering.
  12. Amine Corrosion
      Corrosion in amine treating units from acid gas loading and amine degradation products.
  13. Caustic Corrosion / Gouging
      Localized attack and deposits where caustic concentrates, often under heat flux.
  14. Wet H2S Damage
      Family of hydrogen-charging mechanisms in wet H2S service (blistering, HIC, SOHIC, SSC).
  15. Hydrogen Induced Cracking (HIC)
      Internal stepwise cracking from hydrogen atoms accumulating at inclusions in wet H2S service.
  16. SOHIC
      Stress-oriented HIC — stacked cracking aligned with stress, near welds in wet H2S service.
  17. Sulfide Stress Cracking (SSC)
      Brittle cracking of high-hardness steels in wet H2S — controlled by NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156.
  18. Hydrogen Embrittlement
      Loss of ductility and cracking from absorbed atomic hydrogen, often from welding or cathodic charging.
  19. HTHA (High-Temp Hydrogen Attack)
      Decarburization and fissuring of steel in high-temperature, high-pressure hydrogen — governed by the Nelson curves (API 941).
  20. Chloride SCC
      Cracking of austenitic stainless steels under tensile stress in chloride + temperature environments.
  21. Caustic SCC (Embrittlement)
      Stress corrosion cracking of carbon steel in concentrated caustic above threshold temperatures.
  22. Amine SCC
      Stress corrosion cracking in amine service, typically requiring PWHT for prevention.
  23. Polythionic Acid SCC (PASCC)
      Cracking of sensitized stainless during shutdowns when sulfides react with air and moisture (NACE SP0170).
  24. Creep / Stress Rupture
      Time-dependent deformation and rupture of metals under load at high temperature (fired heater tubes, reformers).
  25. Thermal Fatigue
      Cracking from cyclic thermal stresses — temperature swings, injection points, mixing tees.
  26. Mechanical Fatigue / Vibration
      Crack initiation and growth from cyclic mechanical loading and flow-induced vibration.
  27. Graphitization
      Long-term decomposition of carbides to graphite in carbon / C-1/2Mo steels at temperature, reducing strength.
  28. Temper Embrittlement
      Loss of toughness in Cr-Mo steels held in the 343-593 deg C range over time.
  29. 885 deg F (475 deg C) Embrittlement
      Loss of toughness in ferritic / duplex stainless after exposure near 400-540 deg C.
  30. Sigma Phase Embrittlement
      Brittle intermetallic phase forming in stainless / duplex steels at 565-980 deg C, reducing toughness.
  31. Brittle Fracture
      Sudden fracture below the ductile-to-brittle transition temperature, often at startup / hydrotest.
  32. Refractory Degradation
      Cracking, spalling and loss of fired-equipment refractory leading to hot spots and shell overheating.

SECTION 6: NDE METHODS DEEP-DIVE — 15 METHODS (Division 4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   1. VT — Visual Testing [Direct & remote]
      The foundation of all inspection — surface condition, corrosion, mechanical damage, weld profile. Backed by borescopes and drones.
   2. PT — Penetrant Testing [Surface-breaking]
      Liquid dye reveals surface-breaking flaws on non-porous materials — cracks, porosity, laps.
   3. MT — Magnetic Particle [Surface / near-surface]
      Magnetic flux leakage reveals surface and slightly subsurface flaws in ferromagnetic materials.
   4. UT — Ultrasonic (Thickness) [Volumetric]
      Pulse-echo thickness measurement at CMLs — the workhorse of corrosion monitoring and remaining-life data.
   5. UT — Shear Wave / Flaw [Volumetric]
      Angle-beam detection and sizing of weld flaws and cracks through the wall thickness.
   6. PAUT — Phased Array UT [Advanced volumetric]
      Electronically steered multi-element imaging — fast, high-resolution weld and corrosion mapping.
   7. TOFD — Time of Flight Diffraction [Advanced volumetric]
      Diffraction-based crack sizing with high accuracy, often paired with PAUT for weld inspection.
   8. RT — Radiographic Testing [Volumetric]
      X-ray / gamma imaging of welds and components for internal discontinuities; includes digital RT.
   9. ET — Eddy Current / ECT [Surface & tubing]
      Electromagnetic detection of surface cracks and tube-wall flaws in heat exchangers and condensers.
  10. Guided Wave (GWT / LRUT) [Screening]
      Long-range screening of pipe runs from a single location to flag corrosion for follow-up.
  11. MFL — Magnetic Flux Leakage [Floor / tank]
      Rapid screening of tank floors and tubes for corrosion-driven metal loss.
  12. LT — Leak Testing [Integrity]
      Bubble, pressure-change and tracer-gas methods to confirm pressure-boundary tightness.
  13. IRT — Infrared Thermography [Condition]
      Thermal imaging for refractory hot spots, CUI indicators and electrical connections.
  14. PMI — Positive Material ID [Material]
      XRF / OES verification of alloy composition — preventing material mix-ups in critical service.
  15. Hardness Testing [Material]
      Field hardness to verify PWHT effectiveness and screen for in-service hardening / embrittlement.
