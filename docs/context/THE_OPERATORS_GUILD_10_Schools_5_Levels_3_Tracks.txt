5. Regulatory Alignment Without Overpromising

You can build a powerful academy, but be careful with wording.

Say:

“Aligned with industry expectations, operator qualification concepts, process safety principles, and human-performance practices.”

Do not say:

“Nationally certified nuclear operator”
“PHMSA-certified pipeline operator”
“OSHA-certified process operator”
“Guaranteed job qualification”

For pipelines, PHMSA’s Operator Qualification rule requires pipeline operators to have written OQ programs, covered task lists, and qualification requirements for personnel performing covered tasks. Your guild can help prepare and document training, but the pipeline company’s OQ program controls final qualification.

For OSHA PSM-covered facilities, your content should support operating procedure understanding, process safety training, emergency operations, startup/shutdown, safety systems, and refresher training concepts, but the employer still owns site-specific training and verification.

For nuclear, you can teach nuclear operations fundamentals and simulation judgment, but actual reactor operator and senior reactor operator licensing is governed by NRC requirements.

The Operators Guild™ provides professional development, simulation-based training, competency support, and operational judgment education aligned with industry practices.

Final qualification, licensing, site authorization, and covered-task approval remain the responsibility of the employer, facility, regulatory authority, or applicable operator qualification program.
