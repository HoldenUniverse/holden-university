1. HIT³+ — Holden Inspection & Training Institute

What it is

HIT³+ is your core training school and workforce development platform.

This is where students learn the actual industrial skills: NDE, inspection, mechanical integrity, safety, reporting, field awareness, and job readiness.

HIT is not just a school. It is the foundation of your whole ecosystem.

HIT can include:

NDE/NDT:

* UT
* MT
* PT
* VT
* RT basics
* ET introduction
* Report writing
* Procedure awareness
* Indication evaluation
* Equipment use
* Field documentation

Inspection and mechanical integrity:

* API 510 pressure vessels
* API 570 piping
* API 653 tanks
* API 580 RBI
* API 571 damage mechanisms
* API 577 welding inspection
* API 936 refractory
* API 1169 pipeline inspection
* P&ID reading
* Isometric drawing
* CMLs
* Circuits
* Tmin
* Corrosion monitoring
* Inspection planning

Industrial field skills:

* Safety meetings
* JSA/JHA
* Permit to work
* LOTO awareness
* Confined space awareness
* Working at heights
* Rope access awareness
* Bolting and flange management
* Structural inspection
* Coating awareness
* Turnaround support
* Offshore/onshore work culture

What makes HIT different

Traditional NDE schools usually teach:

method → theory → practical → exam

Your model teaches:

method → field application → judgment → reporting → simulation → employer proof

That is the difference.

HIT is not only asking, “Can the student pass?”

HIT asks:

Can this student survive the field, think clearly, document correctly, communicate professionally, and protect people/assets?

Ideal student

HIT can serve:

* Adults changing careers
* Reentry candidates
* Workforce-transition students
* Rural students
* Young adults needing a trade
* Existing helpers wanting to become technicians
* Painters, scaffold builders, rope techs, welders, and laborers moving into inspection
* Veterans
* Underemployed workers
* Community college students

Best version of HIT

The elite version is:

75–80% online learning
20–25% hands-on labs and field simulations

That allows working adults to keep their jobs while training.

2. Operation Fieldproof

What it is

Operation Fieldproof is your final performance evaluation.

This is not a normal test. This is your “prove it” event.

Students must demonstrate that they can function like real field personnel before graduation.

What students do

They walk into a realistic industrial scenario and must perform tasks such as:

* Attend safety meeting
* Review job package
* Read P&ID
* Read isometric
* Verify equipment number
* Review permit
* Complete JSA
* Perform VT
* Perform MT
* Perform PT
* Perform UT readings
* Identify bad data
* Communicate findings
* Write reports
* Escalate concerns
* Correct documentation errors
* Present final findings
* Defend their decisions

Why it matters

This becomes the student’s proof.

Instead of saying:

“I completed an NDE course.”

They can say:

“I completed a simulated field inspection evaluation where I performed NDE, reviewed drawings, completed reports, handled field changes, and presented findings under pressure.”

That is powerful.

Best commercial use

Operation Fieldproof can become:

* HIT graduation requirement
* Employer demo day
* Student portfolio event
* Recorded resume proof
* Sponsorship showcase
* Workforce development proof
* Community college capstone
* Reentry program proof-of-readiness

6. RiskGateIQ

What it is

RiskGateIQ is your risk decision simulator.

Where IntegrityOS focuses broadly on industrial integrity and operational decisions, RiskGateIQ focuses specifically on risk recognition, prevention, escalation, and consequence.

Versions

You have multiple branches:

RiskGateIQ-OS

Operational safety and industrial risk.

RiskGateIQ-RA

Rope access risk and rescue prevention.

RiskGateIQ-XCC

Extreme crisis command and cross-industry decision-making.

Core concept

RiskGateIQ teaches people to see danger before the incident.

The best worker is not the one who reacts after the failure.

The best worker is the one who prevents the failure.

RiskGateIQ-RA example

“The Rescue That Never Happens”

The goal is not to perform the rescue.

The goal is to identify enough risk early that the rescue never becomes necessary.

Students review:

* Weather
* Rope plan
* Anchor selection
* Rescue plan
* JSA
* Permit
* Equipment
* Crew condition
* Fatigue
* Dropped object exposure
* Communication plan
* Emergency access
* Simultaneous operations

If they miss critical risks, the scenario escalates into:

* Injury
* Rescue
* Fatality
* Dropped object
* Production shutdown
* Regulatory investigation
* Company reputation damage

Why this is powerful

Most safety training is boring.

RiskGateIQ can make safety feel like a real mission.

It creates pressure, suspense, and accountability.

* Whether they are compliant for deployment

11. Greenlight Engine

What it is

Greenlight Engine is your readiness and decision approval system.

It answers:

Is this person, job, inspection, work package, or asset ready to move forward?

Examples

For students:

* Can they move to the next course?
* Are they ready for lab?
* Are they ready for Operation Fieldproof?

For workers:

* Are they ready for deployment?
* Are their certs valid?
* Are documents missing?

For work packages:

* Is the scope complete?
* Are drawings attached?
* Is the permit complete?
* Are hazards reviewed?

For inspection:

* Is the data acceptable?
* Is escalation required?
* Is engineering review needed?

Why it matters

This creates consistency.

Instead of guessing, Greenlight Engine gives a clear status:

* Green: proceed
* Yellow: review required
* Red: stop

12. How Everything Connects

Your ecosystem should work like this:

Phase 1: Recruit

A student discovers HIT through social media, community college, employer partner, church, reentry program, or workforce agency.

Phase 2: Train

They enter HIT³+ and complete coursework.

Phase 3: Practice

They use CertIQ, IQ Suite Pro, CodeIQ, InspectIQ Arena, and IntegrityOS.

Phase 4: Simulate

They enter real industrial scenarios through IntegrityOS and RiskGateIQ.

Phase 5: Prove

They complete Operation Fieldproof.

Phase 6: Verify

Their scores, documents, and readiness go into Deployd.

Phase 7: Employ

Employers review verified candidates.

Phase 8: Operate

D-IMS supports real inspection operations once the student or company enters the field.
