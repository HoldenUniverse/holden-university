THE ENGINEER GUILD™
Advanced Online Simulation Academy for Working Industrial Engineers

Positioning Statement

The Engineer Guild™ is an advanced online engineering command academy for working engineers responsible for designing, operating, troubleshooting, protecting, and extending the life of complex industrial assets.

This is not a traditional engineering school.

This is where working engineers learn how to make high-pressure decisions inside live industrial systems: refineries, chemical plants, LNG facilities, offshore platforms, subsea systems, pipelines, terminals, aerospace systems, power assets, and advanced manufacturing environments.

The Inspector’s Guild trains field judgment.

The Engineer Guild trains engineering command judgment.

1. Core Mission

The Engineer Guild exists to solve one major problem:

Industry has engineers with degrees, but not enough engineers with applied, cross-discipline, field-proven judgment.

A working engineer may understand calculations, but when the asset is vibrating, leaking, corroding, overpressuring, buckling, tripping, or failing during a storm, they need more than formulas.

They need to know:

What is failing?

Why is it failing?

How fast is it failing?

Who needs to be involved?

What code or standard governs the decision?

What are the operational consequences?

Can we safely continue operating?

Do we shut down?

Can we repair?

Can we justify temporary operation?

Can I sign my name to this decision?

That is the heart of The Engineer Guild™.

A. Asset Integrity / Mechanical Integrity Engineers

These are central to the Guild.

Include:

PEI Engineer
Pressure Equipment Integrity engineer responsible for vessels, piping, tanks, relief systems, repairs, inspection plans, degradation tracking, and code compliance.

Fixed Equipment Engineer
Focuses on pressure vessels, heat exchangers, piping systems, columns, tanks, boilers, heaters, and static mechanical assets.

Mechanical Integrity Engineer
Owns MI programs, inspection strategy, repair prioritization, risk ranking, inspection intervals, and integrity documentation.

RBI Engineer
Specializes in Risk-Based Inspection, probability of failure, consequence of failure, inspection effectiveness, and risk-ranking strategies.

Fitness-for-Service Engineer
Uses API 579/ASME FFS-1 style engineering evaluations to determine whether damaged equipment can safely remain in service.

Pressure Vessel Engineer
Specialist in design, rerating, repair, alteration, nozzle loads, fatigue, corrosion allowance, MDMT, MAWP, and pressure boundary decisions.

Piping Engineer
Responsible for piping layout, pipe stress, support design, flexibility, expansion loops, branch connections, vibration, materials, and pressure design.

Tank Engineer
